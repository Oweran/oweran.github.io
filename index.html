About me. A bunch of stuff that you don't have to read

To: Kota

I'm Sean and I started Guruing back in August. I've always played around with code, since I was a kid on the advice of my nerdy father. Mostly Dreamweaver, and creating ugly websites with some great wallpapers and a visitor counter. It was at a time where things were simpler and showing a cursor that looked like a butterfly was acceptable. I then went on to work as online moderator, for 7 years and was remote. From time to time, I was helping out friends with a Wordpress, a Joomla, or a weird website created with I don't exactly know what. I also was the webmaster for a Webzine, which basically meant that I had to fix the stuff that the admin added thinking it would be cool.

I moved from Paris to New Zealand to Brussels, and it's in this city (after a few good Belgian beers) that I decided to go back to school and took night classes and studied cinema. I then spent some time trying to create short movies, editing them, writing them and, sometimes, acting in them. No, I don't have any links, everything was burned in a huge fire.

I then was hired in a startup called Take Eat Easy. We did kind of the same thing as Deliveroo, before being killed by them. It was a great learning experience, as I had the chance to witness the start (when I arrived in customer service we were around 7 or 8 people) and the fall (roughly 35 in my department by the end of it) of a start-up.

Following this experience, I decided to go and live in Ireland, where I spent all my summers as a kid (weird I know, French people are crazy) and moved to Galway where I worked, in customer service again, for Wayfair, before joining Shopify.

Now, I'm at a stage where I want to go back to learning, once again. Shopify is the perfect platform for that, I've never had this opportunity in any of my previous job. Here, you can talk with everyone, and everyone is here to help. And that's what I love.

I looked at the application too quickly and missed the magic sentence: "complete one of the following". Either a static page or a modified Shopify Theme. As I was halfway done, I decided to go overboard and do the two! It was a great learning experience so #noregrets. I also want to thank you. You, the one that read all of that. That took courage.
